Computer engineers, also known as hardware or software engineers, design and develop computer systems.
Hardware engineers are closely related to electrical engineers and focus on the design and installation of
computer chips, processors and other hardware components. Software engineers, on the other hand,
focus more on applying math and computer science to develop and implement software applications.
These branches of computer engineering often go hand in hand,
and professionals in this field typically work among a team of hardware and software engineers.
They apply their knowledge to a wide range of applications, such as communication systems,
microprocessor circuitry and medical equipment, in a variety of industries.
Computer engineers develop computer systems, from hardware to software and everything in between.
You'll need at least a bachelor's degree in a computer science-related field to get started as a computer
engineer, and engineering licensure may improve career prospects in some case.
Computer engineers use knowledge of computer science, math and electrical engineering to help design
computer systems. Individuals who enjoy working with computers and figuring out what makes them work
may enjoy this career.
